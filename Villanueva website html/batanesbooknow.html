1. Sabtang Island
Famous for its stone houses and breathtaking landscapes, Sabtang Island offers stunning beaches and traditional Ivatan culture. A visit to the Sabtang Lighthouse provides panoramic views of the island's picturesque coastline.

2. Marlboro Hills (Raeleh Hills)
Known for its rolling green hills and sweeping views of the ocean, Marlboro Hills is often compared to the Scottish Highlands. It's a perfect spot for hiking and nature photography.

3. Basco Lighthouse
Situated on the island of Batan, this lighthouse offers a majestic view of the entire Basco town, the rolling hills, and the Batanes coastline. A must-visit for stunning scenic views.

4. Ivana Church and San Jose de Ivana Church
These centuries-old Spanish-era churches are among the most beautiful historical sites in Batanes, featuring unique architecture and a glimpse into the island’s colonial past.

5. Nakabuang Arch
Located on Sabtang Island, this natural limestone arch is one of Batanes’ most iconic sights. It’s a serene spot with crystal-clear waters and dramatic rock formations.

6. Chinapalo Hill
This hill provides a panoramic view of the surrounding islands and is particularly famous for its vibrant green slopes, which are especially stunning during the rainy season.

7. Vayang Rolling Hills
One of the most photogenic locations in Batanes, Vayang Rolling Hills features sweeping, green hills that seem to roll into the horizon. The hill offers panoramic views of the sea and other islands.

8. Dipnaysupuan Japanese Tunnel
A historic tunnel from the Japanese occupation during World War II, the Dipnaysupuan Tunnel offers insight into the island’s war history and makes for a fascinating visit.

9. Valugan Boulder Beach
This unique beach is covered with large smooth boulders, creating a striking contrast against the crystal-clear waters. It is ideal for a peaceful retreat, photography, or a scenic walk.

10. Sabtang's Traditional Ivatan Houses
The Ivatan people have a distinct architectural style, using materials like limestone, coral, and cogon grass. These traditional homes, with their thick walls, are built to withstand the region's harsh climatic conditions.
