Open Window Flower

A Concept in painting theory that suggests that the frame of a painting should be considered like an open window. This concept was introduced by Leon Battista Alberti's 1435 treatise De Pictura. It was defining concept for painting theories, as well as for architecture and moving pictures.

Alice Hoschede in the Garden

It is an Oil painting by Claude Monet that depicts his Lover, Alice Hoschede, sitting in his garden at Wetheuil in 1881. It has a Impressionist style with vibrant color palette and expressive brush strokes. It is considered one of monet's most ambitious works.

Nympheas

It is called Wate Lilies, it is a series of approximately 250 oil painting by french impressionist Claude Monet. The painting depict his flower garden at his home in Giverny, and were the main focus of his artistic production during the last thirty years of his life.
